My Dumb App

  • Outline of canvas object in yellow, background color of canvas is white. This is controlled from the HTML level.
  • The root interactor bridges the HTML and tropical worlds. It shifts its content inward by 5 pix, paints background melon colored and rounds its corners
  • DumbParent fills its upper left quadrant with cornsilk (sort of brownish) as well as showing its border in same color.
    Note that it tries to just do a simple filled rectangle but since its parent (the root interactor) set the clipping path with rounded rects, the upper left remains rounded
  • Blue rectangles (royal blue and dodger blue) are instances of DumbChild. They just fill their entiring bounding rect with a color.
    The blue rectangles respond to Click()so they only change *if* the mouse is released inside their bounds (plus of course starting inside their bounds) They change from filled to stroked.
  • The icon in circle is actually a composite. The Porthole type masks its child, whatever it is, with an arc (360 degrees in this case). We use a the IconLeaf as it's child, but the masking removes the part outside the boundary of Porthole.
    You can see that the icon is square by looking at the HTML of this page and seeing the hidden icon at the bottom. It must be in the page to be drawn inside a tropical interactor, but we hide it from the HTML viewer.
  • You can drag the icon around. It defaults to picking only itself, not its children. You may want to place it partially obscuring a blue rectangle and observing the effect on the click behavior of the rectangle.
  • The squares with an X in it the upper and lower right demonstrate how to override the "default draw" of the entire system--and it's a throwback to an older time...
  • The squares with an X are "later" in the child list than the draggable porthole so dragging the porthole appears to go "under" these interactors but over the blue squares.
